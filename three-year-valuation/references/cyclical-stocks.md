# 周期股分析分支

Use this reference when the business depends heavily on commodity prices, capacity cycles, macro demand, freight rates, real-estate/construction cycles, inventory cycles, interest rates, or policy-driven supply/demand.

## 1. First Principle

For cyclical stocks, current earnings are often the least reliable input. Peak earnings make P/E look low and trough earnings make P/E look high or meaningless. Replace "year-three profit" with "year-three or mid-cycle earning power" and require a larger safety margin.

## 2. Preferred Valuation Methods

Use several lenses together:

1. Normalized earnings multiple:
   - Estimate mid-cycle net profit or EBITDA across a full cycle.
   - Apply a lower multiple than for durable compounders.
   - Use EV/EBIT, EV/EBITDA, or EV/NOPAT when leverage and depreciation distort P/E.
2. Asset/replacement-cost lens:
   - Compare EV with replacement cost, net asset value, proven reserves, capacity value, or liquidation downside.
   - Useful for steel, cement, shipping, mining, chemicals, and other capital-intensive sectors.
3. Through-cycle DCF or NAV:
   - Model volume, commodity price/spread, cash cost, sustaining capex, working capital, and terminal assumptions.
   - For resource assets, use reserves, mine life, production schedule, cost curve, and reclamation/closure obligations.
4. Relative cycle valuation:
   - Compare the company's EV/capacity, EV/reserve, EV/ton, EV/EBITDA, P/B, and margins versus peers at similar cycle points.
   - Do not compare trough multiples with peak multiples without normalizing the cycle phase.

Avoid using current P/E alone.

## 3. Cycle Position Checklist

Identify where the industry is in the cycle:

- Capacity: new capacity pipeline, shutdowns, utilization, bottlenecks, environmental/policy constraints.
- Demand: downstream demand drivers, construction/manufacturing/export indicators, customer inventory.
- Price/spread: commodity price, product spread, raw material/energy cost, freight cost.
- Inventory: company inventory, channel inventory, exchange warehouse inventory if relevant.
- Cost curve: company position versus peers; low-cost producers survive troughs and benefit most from recovery.
- Balance sheet: net debt, maturity wall, covenant pressure, refinancing risk.
- Capex cycle: whether the industry is adding capacity because peak profits invited competition.
- Management behavior: buybacks/dividends at troughs versus empire-building at peaks.

## 4. Data Requirements

Minimum useful data:

- At least one full industry cycle; often 10 years of annual reports if available.
- Latest annual report, interim report, and quarterly reports to locate current cycle position.
- Segment revenue, volume, average selling price, unit cost, gross margin, EBIT/EBITDA, capex, depreciation, working capital, and debt maturity.
- Industry capacity, utilization, inventory, price/spread series, cost curve, import/export, and policy changes.
- Peer financials across the same cycle.
- Management guidance, analyst forecasts, and recent news, used as inputs to test assumptions rather than as conclusions.

## 5. Estimating Year-Three or Mid-Cycle Profit

Build a driver model:

```text
Revenue = volume × average selling price
Gross profit = volume × unit spread
Operating profit = gross profit - fixed cost - variable opex
Net profit = operating profit - interest - tax - minority interest
Free cash flow = net profit + depreciation - sustaining capex - working capital increase
```

Use at least three scenarios:

- Trough: weak demand, low utilization, poor spread, refinancing pressure.
- Mid-cycle: normalized demand, normalized spread, sustainable utilization.
- Peak: high utilization and strong spread, but assume reversion.

Treat "peak profit × low P/E" as a warning sign, not as cheapness.

## 6. Buy Discipline for Cyclicals

Be stricter than with high-quality compounders:

- Prefer low-cost leaders with strong balance sheets.
- Require survival through a trough without dilution or debt distress.
- Demand larger discount to mid-cycle value.
- Use smaller position sizing unless cycle understanding is unusually strong.
- Predefine sell/trim logic when margins and sentiment normalize or overshoot.
